Why This Project Exists

Project Background

Saint Joseph Gahanga Parish currently manages all records on paper — a system that is slow, error-prone, and impossible to query at scale. Two internal planning meetings in 2024 produced a clear picture of what the digital system must do. AbyTech Hub is here to build it.

The Current Problem

  • Parishioner records are kept in paper ledgers — no search, no backup
  • Sacrament history must be looked up manually per person
  • Financial collections are tracked in notebooks with no audit trail
  • No way to instantly know how many Christians are in the parish
  • Group memberships and positions change but records stay outdated
  • Reports for the diocese require days of manual counting

What the System Will Do

  • Every parishioner registered digitally with full history
  • All sacraments logged per person — searchable in seconds
  • Every collection, donation, and fee tracked with a full audit trail
  • Real-time dashboard showing total Christians and all breakdowns
  • Group rosters always current — updates reflect instantly
  • Reports generated in one click — no manual counting

Data Privacy Constraint

In prior parish meetings, a critical concern was raised: the parish must not rely on foreign third-party software platforms that would store parishioner data outside local control. The system AbyTech Hub will build is custom-built and locally hosted — all data stays within the parish's own infrastructure.

System Architecture

Parish Data Structure

Every record in the system is anchored to an individual household within a 5-level organizational hierarchy. Moving up the tree aggregates data for reporting at each level.

Parish Level 1
Top-level administrative unit. All data rolls up to this level.
Chapel Zone Level 2
Geographic division of the parish — each with its own chapel or sub-location.
Family Group Cluster Level 3
A cluster of multiple family groups within a chapel zone.
Family Cluster Level 4
A group of related households within a family group cluster.
Individual Household Level 5 — Base Unit
The base unit of the system. All Christian records, sacraments, and financial entries are attached here.

"Think of it like an org chart for the entire parish — from the top-level parish down to every individual family. Every entry in the system traces back to a specific household."

What Will Be Built

8 System Modules — Full Scope

The Gahanga Management System will be built in 8 modules. Each module covers a distinct area of parish operations. All 8 modules were confirmed at this first meeting.

1. Christian Management

Register, edit, transfer, and manage every Catholic household in the parish using the household registration form.

2. Fund Management

Track all parish income — Sunday collections, Mass bookings, subscriptions, donations, and special-occasion fees.

3. Publications

Upload and distribute announcements, reports, strategic plans, action plans, and upcoming event calendars.

4. Group Management

Create and manage all parish groups — priests, councils, commissions, youth groups — with full member and role tracking.

5. Sacraments

Track all 6 Catholic sacraments per person — from Baptism to Holy Orders — with class and exam records where required.

6. Parish Website

A public-facing website for the parish — information, events, and announcements accessible online.

7. Reports & Dashboard

Real-time dashboards showing total Christians broken down by gender, age, sacrament, family status, and education level.

8. Integrations

Connect to SMS, mobile money (MOMO), bank payments, local government systems, and social media platforms.

Module 1

Christian Management

The Christian Management module is the foundation of the entire system. Every other module — sacraments, funds, groups, reports — traces back to a household record stored here. All current paper household registration forms are replaced by this digital equivalent.

How It Works

Parish staff register every Catholic household in the parish using a digital form that mirrors the existing paper household registration form. When a household is created, the system automatically places it at the correct position in the 5-level hierarchy — linked to its Chapel Zone, Family Group Cluster, and Family Cluster. Once registered, the household has a permanent profile page that consolidates all information about that family: every member listed, all sacraments they have received, their financial transaction history with the parish, and which parish groups they belong to. Everything about one family is visible from a single screen.

Staff can open any household record at any time to correct or update information — names, marital status, address, member additions or removals. Changes take effect immediately across the whole system. When a family physically moves to a different zone or chapel area, their record is transferred to the new location without duplicating it — the complete history of that household follows them. If a family leaves the parish entirely, their record is deactivated rather than deleted. It no longer appears in active reports and counts, but the data is retained in the database for historical audit purposes.

Module 2

Parish Fund Management

The Fund Management module records every financial transaction that passes through the parish — from Sunday Mass offerings to special occasion fees. Every entry is categorized, dated, and permanently stored. Paper collection notebooks are fully replaced by a searchable, auditable digital ledger.

How It Works

When any financial collection takes place — whether at a Sunday Mass, a wedding, or a special fundraising event — a parish staff member records the entry in the system. Each entry is assigned to one of the defined collection categories, given an amount, a date, and the name of whoever collected it. The system builds a running ledger that can be searched and filtered at any time. Totals per category, per date range, or per zone can be generated without any manual addition.

For digital payments through MOMO or bank (see Module 8 — Integrations), the payment is automatically recorded as a new entry in this module the moment it is confirmed — no manual data entry required.

Module 3

Publications

The Publications module is the parish's internal and public document management system. All official communication — whether a Sunday notice, a committee report, or a multi-year strategic plan — is uploaded, stored, and made accessible here.

How It Works

An authorized staff member or committee secretary logs into the system and uploads a document or types an announcement directly. Every item is given a content type, a date, and an author. The system organizes content by type so that parishioners and staff can quickly find what they are looking for — all announcements in one place, all reports in another, all action plans together. Nothing gets lost in a pile of paper or a shared folder with no structure.

Published content is visible on the parish's public website (Module 6) depending on whether the staff member marks it as public or internal-only. Internal documents such as committee reports and financial plans remain accessible only to authorized users within the system.

Module 4

Parish Group Management

The Group Management module keeps a live, accurate record of every formal group operating within the parish — who belongs to each group, what role they hold, and how leadership is currently structured. There is no longer a risk of outdated rosters or unrecorded changes.

How It Works

Each group in the parish is created as a record in the system with a name and a group type. Once the group exists, members are added from the existing household database — meaning the system links directly to the person's household record rather than creating a separate entry for them. Every member is given a role within that group at the time they are added. When a person takes on a new position or leaves a group, the roster is updated immediately. The system always reflects current membership — not what was true six months ago.

Because members are linked from household records, the system can cross-reference a person's group involvement from their household profile, and can show a group's full member list from the group's own page. No duplication of data.

Module 5

Sacrament Management

The Sacrament Management module creates a permanent, searchable record of every sacrament received by every person in the parish. All 6 Catholic sacraments are tracked here, each with its own set of required data and its own rules about preparation classes and examinations.

How It Works

When a person is preparing to receive a sacrament, a staff member opens their record and creates a new sacrament entry. The system presents the correct input form based on which sacrament is being recorded — each sacrament collects different information, and the system enforces this automatically. For sacraments that require a preparation class (such as First Communion or Confirmation), the system records the teacher, the school or location, and the start and end dates of the class, as well as whether the person sat and passed the exam. For sacraments that do not require preparation (such as Anointing of the Sick), those fields are not shown.

Staff can search for any person and immediately see every sacrament they have received, when it was administered, and who the teacher or officiant was. The system does not allow the same sacrament to be recorded twice for the same person, preventing duplicate entries.

Module 6

Parish Website

The Parish Website module is the public face of Saint Joseph Gahanga Parish on the internet. Anyone — parishioners, visitors, or people looking to join the parish — can access it without needing a login or account.

How It Works

The website is managed entirely from within the same administration dashboard used for all other modules. There is no separate content management system to log into. When a staff member publishes an announcement in Module 3 (Publications) and marks it as public, it automatically appears on the parish website. The same applies to upcoming events and the parish programme schedule. This means the website always reflects the latest information posted by staff — there is no need to update two separate systems.

The website displays: parish identity and history, contact details and location, Mass times and the weekly schedule, current announcements, upcoming events, and the parish's published programme. The full detailed specification for the website's design, pages, and features will be submitted by Mr. KARANGWA Joseph as a separate requirements document.

Module 7

Reports & Dashboard

The Reports and Dashboard module gives parish leadership an instant, accurate view of the state of the parish — total Christians, every demographic breakdown, and financial summaries — without any manual counting or spreadsheet work.

How It Works

The dashboard reads directly from the household and sacrament records stored in the system and calculates totals in real time. Parish leadership open the dashboard and immediately see the current total number of Christians registered across the whole parish. From there, they can apply filters to break the data down by any combination of the dimensions below. All data is live — when a new household is registered or a sacrament is recorded, the dashboard reflects it immediately. Reports can be exported and printed for submission to the diocese or for committee meetings.

The dashboard is filterable by Chapel Zone, by commission, or at the whole-parish level — so zone leaders can see figures for their area, and the Parish Priest can see the full picture.

Module 8

Integrations

The Integrations module connects the parish management system to the external services that the parish already uses or needs to use — digital payments, text messaging, government systems, and social media. All of these connections are managed from within the same dashboard, not from separate apps.

How It Works

Each integration is a two-way or one-way connection between the parish system and an external platform. Once configured, staff do not need to open a separate app or website to use these services — the action happens from within the parish system itself.

SMS Gateway
Staff compose a message in the dashboard and select the recipients — an individual household, a group, a chapel zone, or the entire parish. The system sends the text message directly to each person's registered phone number. No phone app required. Used for announcements, event reminders, and urgent communications.
Digital Payments — MTN MOMO & Bank Transfer
Parishioners pay offerings, Mass booking fees, subscriptions, and other dues digitally using MTN Mobile Money or a bank transfer. When a payment is confirmed, the system automatically creates the corresponding entry in the Fund Management module (Module 2) — no manual data entry needed. The parish receives the payment and the record is created in one transaction.
Local Government Systems
The system can exchange data with Rwandan local government and citizen-record systems. This allows the parish to cross-verify household and identity information against official records and to submit required reports to government agencies directly from the system.
Other Religious Organizations
The system supports data sharing and cooperation with other recognized churches and religious bodies in Rwanda — for example, verifying sacrament records when a parishioner transfers from another parish.
Social Media — X (Twitter) · WhatsApp · Facebook
When a staff member publishes an announcement or event in Module 3, they can choose to simultaneously share it to the parish's X, WhatsApp, and Facebook accounts — all from within the dashboard. The content is posted automatically without the staff member needing to open those platforms separately or copy and paste the text. This keeps all parish social media channels current with no extra effort.
