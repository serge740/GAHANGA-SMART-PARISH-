Why This Project Exists

Project Background

Saint Joseph Gahanga Parish currently manages all records on paper — a system that is slow, error-prone, and impossible to query at scale. Two internal planning meetings in 2024 produced a clear picture of what the digital system must do. AbyTech Hub is here to build it.

The Current Problem

  • Parishioner records are kept in paper ledgers — no search, no backup
  • Sacrament history must be looked up manually per person
  • Financial collections are tracked in notebooks with no audit trail
  • No way to instantly know how many Christians are in the parish
  • Group memberships and positions change but records stay outdated
  • Reports for the diocese require days of manual counting

What the System Will Do

  • Every parishioner registered digitally with full history
  • All sacraments logged per person — searchable in seconds
  • Every collection, donation, and fee tracked with a full audit trail
  • Real-time dashboard showing total Christians and all breakdowns
  • Group rosters always current — updates reflect instantly
  • Reports generated in one click — no manual counting

Data Privacy Constraint

In prior parish meetings, a critical concern was raised: the parish must not rely on foreign third-party software platforms that would store parishioner data outside local control. The system AbyTech Hub will build is custom-built and locally hosted — all data stays within the parish's own infrastructure.

System Architecture

Parish Data Structure

Every record in the system is anchored to an individual household within a 5-level organizational hierarchy. Moving up the tree aggregates data for reporting at each level.

Parish Level 1
Top-level administrative unit. All data rolls up to this level.
Chapel Zone Level 2
Geographic division of the parish — each with its own chapel or sub-location.
Family Group Cluster Level 3
A cluster of multiple family groups within a chapel zone.
Family Cluster Level 4
A group of related households within a family group cluster.
Individual Household Level 5 — Base Unit
The base unit of the system. All Christian records, sacraments, and financial entries are attached here.

"Think of it like an org chart for the entire parish — from the top-level parish down to every individual family. Every entry in the system traces back to a specific household."

What Will Be Built

8 System Modules — Full Scope

The Gahanga Management System will be built in 8 modules. Each module covers a distinct area of parish operations. All 8 modules were confirmed at this first meeting.

1. Christian Management

Register, edit, transfer, and manage every Catholic household in the parish using the household registration form.

2. Fund Management

Track all parish income — Sunday collections, Mass bookings, subscriptions, donations, and special-occasion fees.

3. Publications

Upload and distribute announcements, reports, strategic plans, action plans, and upcoming event calendars.

4. Group Management

Create and manage all parish groups — priests, councils, commissions, youth groups — with full member and role tracking.

5. Sacraments

Track all 6 Catholic sacraments per person — from Baptism to Holy Orders — with class and exam records where required.

6. Parish Website

A public-facing website for the parish — information, events, and announcements accessible online.

7. Reports & Dashboard

Real-time dashboards showing total Christians broken down by gender, age, sacrament, family status, and education level.

8. Integrations

Connect to SMS, mobile money (MOMO), bank payments, local government systems, and social media platforms.

Module 1

Christian Management

The core of the system. Every Catholic household in the parish will be registered using the household registration form. This module is the foundation that all other modules reference.

Use Cases

1

Register a New Household

A new Catholic family fills in the household registration form. The system records their household at the correct level in the 5-level hierarchy and links them to their chapel zone.

2

Edit & Update Household Records

Parish staff can correct or update any household information — names, address, family status, or member changes — at any time.

3

Transfer a Household

When a family moves to a different zone or chapel area, their record is transferred — not duplicated. Full history moves with them.

4

View Full Household Profile

One-click access to the complete household profile — all members, sacraments received, financial history, and group memberships in a single view.

5

Deactivate a Household

If a family leaves the parish or a record is no longer active, it can be deactivated — data is preserved for reporting history but removed from active lists.

Module 2

Parish Fund Management

Every franc that enters the parish — from Sunday collections to wedding fees — will be logged, categorized, and traceable. No more notebooks. Every transaction has a record.

Regular Collections

  • Church offerings collected at Mass
  • Weekly Sunday Mass offerings
  • Subscriptions / membership dues
  • Membership fees

Special & One-off Entries

  • Mass bookings
  • Funeral Mass fees
  • Wedding Mass fees
  • Baptism celebration fees
  • Donations
  • Miscellaneous income
Module 3

Publications

A central place to upload and distribute all official parish documents — from Sunday announcements to the annual strategic plan. Everything findable in one place.

Announcements

Notices, alerts, and weekly bulletins.

Reports

Quarterly commission reports, annual meeting reports, and academic year summaries.

Strategic Plan

The parish's long-term strategic plan document.

Action Plans

Annual action plans per commission and the overall parish plan d'action.

Upcoming Events

Advance calendar of all planned events and celebrations.

Parish Schedule

The running weekly and monthly schedule of parish activities.

Module 4

Parish Group Management

All parish groups — from the main council to youth clubs and Catholic Action families — will be registered in the system with their full membership rosters and leadership positions always current.

Groups to Register

Leadership & Governance

  • Priests
  • Parish Council
  • Finance Committee
  • Commissions

Community Groups

  • Chapel Zone Groups
  • Youth Groups
  • Family Cluster Groups
  • Catholic Action Families

What Is Captured Per Member

For every member of every group: Full name · Personal identification details · Position / Role in the group (President, Secretary, Treasurer, Member, etc.)

Module 5

Sacrament Management

Every Catholic sacrament will be tracked per person. Each of the 6 sacraments has its own registration workflow, with different class and exam requirements depending on the sacrament and the age of the recipient.

Adults (7 years +) Yes Yes ID · Teacher · School · Start & End Date
2 Eucharist All parishioners Yes Yes ID · Teacher · School · Start & End Date
3 Confirmation All parishioners Yes Yes ID · Teacher · School · Start & End Date
4 Marriage All parishioners Yes Yes ID · Teacher · School · Start & End Date
5 Anointing of the Sick Sick persons N/A N/A Patient ID · Priest ID · Date
6 Holy Orders Ordained priests Seminary record — ID · Seminary name · Ordination date · Religious Order

Parish Website

A public-facing website for Saint Joseph Gahanga Parish — accessible to all parishioners and the wider community online. Detailed website specification to be provided by Mr. KARANGWA Joseph before the second meeting.

Module 7

Reports & Dashboard

Parish leadership will have access to a real-time dashboard that shows the total number of Christians and a full breakdown across every relevant dimension — in one click, not days of counting.

Dashboard Dimensions

Included in Phase 1

  • Total Christians — whole parish
  • Breakdown: Women / Men / Children / Youth
  • By sacrament received: Baptized, Communion, Confirmed, Married, Anointed, Ordained
  • By family status: two-parent, widows, single parents, separated, remarried
  • By education level
  • Per chapel zone, per commission, whole-parish aggregate

Later Phases

  • Historical trend charts (year-over-year growth)
  • Diocese-level consolidated reporting
  • Predictive analytics
  • Automated report scheduling (email delivery)
Module 8

Integrations

The system will connect to external platforms to enable digital payments, mass notifications, government data exchange, and social media publishing — all from within the parish management dashboard.

SMS Gateway
Send text message alerts and announcements directly to parishioners' phones from within the system. No third-party app required.
Digital Payments
Parishioners can pay offerings, booking fees, and subscriptions digitally via MTN Mobile Money (MOMO) or bank transfer.
Local Government
Integration with Rwandan local government and citizen-data systems for cross-verification and official reporting.
Other Denominations
Data cooperation and exchange with other recognized religious organizations in Rwanda.
Social Media — X (Twitter) · WhatsApp · Facebook
Parish administrators can publish announcements and events directly to all social platforms from within the system dashboard — no separate logins or copy-pasting needed.
